Tips for Healthier Layers and More Eggs


By Alawusa Heritage Fish & Farms


INTRODUCTION


Eggs are one of the most consumed and affordable sources of animal protein in Nigeria. For poultry farmers—whether small-scale or commercial—maintaining the health of your laying hens (layers) is the key to maximizing egg quality and quantity. But successful egg production isn’t about luck — it’s about consistency in feed, housing, lighting, and hygiene.


1. Provide a Balanced and Nutritionally Complete Diet


Feeding your birds the right diet is the first step to healthy layers.

Why Nutrition Matters:


Calcium is essential for strong eggshells.


Protein supports egg production and muscle maintenance.


Energy helps with general productivity and health.


Poor feeding leads to thin-shelled or shell-less eggs, weak birds, and reduced output.


What To Feed:


Use quality layer mash or pellets with:


  • 16–18% crude protein

  • 3–4% calcium(from sources like oyster shells or limestone)

  • Essential vitamins and minerals(Vitamin D3, phosphorus, etc.)

    • Provide grit or crushed stones to aid digestion.


    Ensure clean, cool water is always available — dehydration slows down laying.



    TIP: You can mix crushed snail shells, bone meal, or charcoal to supplement calcium naturally.

    2. Ensure Adequate Lighting (14–16 Hours Daily)


    Layers are photoperiod-sensitive — their laying cycle depends on the number of hours of light they receive daily.

    Why Light Is Important:


  • Stimulates the pituitary gland to produce hormones that trigger egg laying.

  • Keeps birds active and feeding.

    • What To Do:


  • Provide at least 14–16 hours of light daily, especially in the rainy season or during Harmattan.

  • Use energy-saving bulbs or LED lights inside the coop.

  • Ensure light intensity is around 10–20 lux (enough to read a newspaper at bird level).

  • Use timers for automated lighting schedules if possible.


    • NOTE: Do not suddenly switch light hours. Gradually increase or reduce light to avoid stress.

    3. Maintain a Clean, Dry, and Well-Ventilated Coop


    Poor coop hygiene is one of the most common causes of disease and low egg production.

    Why Sanitation Matters:


  • Reduces disease risk (like coccidiosis, E. coli, Newcastle).

  • Prevents buildup of ammonia gas, which causes respiratory distress.

  • Ensures birds are comfortable and stress-free.

    • What To Do:


  • Use deep litter system with materials like wood shavings, rice husks, or sawdust — change regularly.

  • Sweep droppings, remove leftover feed, and clean drinkers daily.

  • Install cross ventilation: use mesh wire, windows, or vents to allow airflow.

  • Disinfect the coop weekly with poultry-safe disinfectants.


    • PRO TIP: Sprinkle lime or charcoal under the litter to reduce odor and moisture.

    4. Prevent & Manage Stress


    Stress causes hormonal imbalances that reduce egg-laying rates.

    Stress factors include:


  • Loud noise or sudden movements

  • Overcrowding

  • Rough handling

  • Poor weather conditions (too hot, too cold)

  • Changes in feed or environment

    • What To Do:


  • Keep 4–5 birds per square meter.

  • Avoid mixing new birds with older ones without quarantine.

  • Maintain a routine: feed at the same time, clean regularly, and keep the coop peaceful.

  • Install protective fencing to keep out predators (snakes, dogs, hawks).


    • 5. Health Monitoring and Vaccination


    Layers need regular health checks and protection from common poultry diseases.

    What to do:


  • Deworm every 2–3 months using poultry-approved dewormers.

  • Observe birds for signs of sickness: drooping wings, loss of appetite, ruffled feathers.

  • Follow a proper vaccination schedule (Newcastle, Gumboro, Fowl Pox, etc.)

  • Work with a vet or agri-extension officer for guidance.


    • Healthy birds = safe, profitable eggs.

    Final Thoughts


    Raising layers isn't just about feeding and waiting — it's about creating the right environment for consistent egg production. At Alawusa Heritage Fish & Farms, we blend decades of traditional know-how with modern practices to raise healthy, happy hens that lay quality eggs Nigerian families can trust. Whether you're just starting or managing a growing flock, apply these tips to make your poultry operation cleaner, healthier, and more productive.

    Action Benefit
    Feed high-protein, calcium-rich diet Strong shells, more eggs
    Provide 14–16 hours of light daily Triggers consistent laying
    Clean coop & dry litter Prevents disease & improves comfort
    Prevent stress & overcrowding Maintains steady production
    Monitor health & vaccinate Keeps flock safe and productive
