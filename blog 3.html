1. Design Your Pig Housing for Easy Cleaning


The way you build your piggery affects how well you manage waste.

What a good pig house should include:


  • Sloped concrete floors so urine and water drain easily.

  • A gutter or channel leading to a waste pit or septic tank.

  • A raised or slatted floor system (optional) that allows droppings to fall below for easy collection.

  • Separate sleeping, feeding, and waste zones to keep pigs clean and reduce contamination.

    2. Clean Pens Daily and Disinfect Weekly


    Cleaning is your best defense against bad smells, flies, and diseases.

    Daily cleaning routine:


  • Remove solid waste (manure) and leftover feed.

  • Scrub the floor with water and a mild disinfectant.

  • Clear drains and make sure water is flowing freely.

  • Change or add dry bedding (like sawdust or rice husk) in farrowing pens.

    • Weekly Routine:


  • Use lime, bleach, or natural disinfectants to kill bacteria.

  • Burn or bury heavy waste far from the farm.

  • Remove urine-soaked litter to avoid ammonia buildup.


    • PRO TIP: Use EM (Effective Microorganisms) solutions to break down waste and control odor naturally.

    3. Compost Pig Waste into Organic Fertilizer


    Instead of throwing pig waste away, turn it into compost that can fertilize crops or be sold.

    How to compost pig manure:


  • Mix manure with dry plant material (sawdust, dry leaves, straw) in a 3:1 ratio.

    Waste attracts flies and rodents ‚Äî which spread disease and increase odor problems.

    What to do:


  • Cover waste pits or compost areas with mesh or tarpaulin.

  • Use fly traps, lime powder, or poultry dust to reduce fly breeding.

  • Store feed properly to avoid attracting rats.

  • Seal pen cracks or holes to block pest entry.

    For larger farms, a biogas digester can turn pig waste into:

  • Cooking gas (methane)

  • Organic slurry for crop farms


    • How It Works:


  • Manure flows into an airtight tank.

  • Bacteria break it down and release gas.

  • The gas is stored and piped to kitchens or stoves.
